# 30 More Uncommon Insects for Bug Explorer (Axel curation, 2026-06-10)

The current 30 leans heavy on flagship beauty bugs (Atlas Moth, Goliath Beetle, Glasswing) and butterflies/moths/mantises. The next 30 expands into:

- Underrepresented orders (Hemiptera assassins, Neuroptera lacewings, Phasmida sticks)
- Aquatic insects (giant water bugs, dobsonflies, mayflies)
- Social engineers (leafcutter ants, weaver ants, honeypots, bullets)
- Tiny extremes (fairyflies = smallest insects ever)
- Pure visual showstoppers (giraffe weevil, lantern bug, harlequin beetle)

Total order distribution after the merge: butterflies/moths drop from 27% → 17% of total; Hemiptera rises from 7% → 15%; Hymenoptera 10% → 17%; aquatic representation goes from 0% → 8%.

## Aquatic predators (5)

1. **Giant Water Bug** (*Lethocerus americanus*) — uncommon — 12cm. Hunts frogs, fish, baby snakes underwater. Bite called "the worst pain in entomology" by some. Order: Hemiptera.
2. **Eastern Dobsonfly** (*Corydalus cornutus*) — uncommon — males grow 4cm tusks they CAN'T bite with (used to court females, not eat). 18cm wingspan. Order: Megaloptera.
3. **Water Strider** (*Gerris remigis*) — common — walks on water using surface tension; tiny hairs on legs trap air. Order: Hemiptera.
4. **Mayfly** (*Ephemera danica*) — uncommon — adults can't eat; live 1 day to mate, die. Mass emergences look like snow. Order: Ephemeroptera.
5. **Diving Beetle** (*Dytiscus marginalis*) — common — carries an air bubble under its wing case; goes 36 hours underwater on one breath. Order: Coleoptera.

## Tiny extremes (4)

6. **Fairyfly** (*Dicopomorpha echmepterygis*) — legendary — smallest insect ever discovered, 0.139mm. Lives inside another insect's eggs. Order: Hymenoptera.
7. **Springtail** (*Anurida maritima*) — common — antarctic-tolerant; survives being frozen solid then thawed. Older than dinosaurs (400M years). Order: Collembola (technically not insect, but listed here for the wonder).
8. **Brazilian Treehopper** (*Bocydium globulare*) — rare — wears a ridiculous helicopter-rotor crown of black globes nobody can fully explain. Looks designed by an alien. Order: Hemiptera.
9. **Mantispid / Mantis Lacewing** (*Mantispa styriaca*) — uncommon — convergent evolution made it look like a mantis but it's actually a lacewing relative. Larva sneaks into spider egg sacs. Order: Neuroptera.

## Underground & nocturnal hunters (4)

10. **Mole Cricket** (*Gryllotalpa gryllotalpa*) — common — front legs are literal shovels; sings from a tuned trumpet-shaped burrow that amplifies its call across fields. Order: Orthoptera.
11. **Antlion** (*Myrmeleon formicarius*) — uncommon — larva digs sand pits, ambushes ants from below; adult is a delicate dragonfly-like flier. Order: Neuroptera.
12. **Glow-worm** (*Lampyris noctiluca*) — uncommon — flightless females glow green from the abdomen to attract winged males. Bioluminescent love letters. Order: Coleoptera.
13. **New Zealand Glow-worm** (*Arachnocampa luminosa*) — rare — fly larva that lights up cave ceilings; "starlit" caves of Waitomo are actually maggot lights. Order: Diptera.

## Visual showstoppers (5)

14. **Lantern Bug** (*Pyrops candelaria*) — rare — has an enormous false snout shaped like a peanut. Old folklore said it glowed; it doesn't, but the "lantern" name stuck. Order: Hemiptera.
15. **Giraffe Weevil** (*Trachelophorus giraffa*) — rare — Madagascar endemic; males have a comically long red neck used for combat and leaf-rolling. Order: Coleoptera.
16. **Harlequin Beetle** (*Acrocinus longimanus*) — uncommon — South American; arms longer than its body, marbled red/black/yellow pattern. Order: Coleoptera.
17. **Frog-legged Leaf Beetle** (*Sagra buqueti*) — rare — iridescent jewel-tones, with absurdly oversized hind legs that look stolen from a frog. Order: Coleoptera.
18. **Hercules Moth** (*Coscinocera hercules*) — legendary — Australian/New Guinea giant; female has 27cm wingspan, largest by surface area of any moth. Order: Lepidoptera. (Different from Hercules BEETLE we already have.)

## Social engineers (5)

19. **Leafcutter Ant** (*Atta cephalotes*) — uncommon — doesn't eat the leaves; farms fungus on them inside underground cathedrals. Civilization-scale agriculture, smaller than your fingernail. Order: Hymenoptera.
20. **Weaver Ant** (*Oecophylla smaragdina*) — uncommon — sews leaves into nests using its own larvae as living glue guns. Used as biological pest control for 1700+ years. Order: Hymenoptera.
21. **Honeypot Ant** (*Myrmecocystus mexicanus*) — rare — specialized "repletes" hang from cave ceilings as living food storage; their abdomens swell to translucent amber spheres. Order: Hymenoptera.
22. **Bullet Ant** (*Paraponera clavata*) — uncommon — top of the Schmidt sting pain index (4.0+); Sateré-Mawé initiation rituals use gloves filled with them. Order: Hymenoptera.
23. **Termite Queen** (*Macrotermes bellicosus*) — uncommon — 5cm sausage-shaped, lives 50 years, lays an egg every 3 seconds. The colony she founds builds 8m mounds with internal AC. Order: Blattodea.

## Big drama (4)

24. **Titan Beetle** (*Titanus giganteus*) — legendary — Amazonian; up to 17cm body length, jaws can snap a pencil. Adults don't eat; live to mate. Order: Coleoptera.
25. **Lubber Grasshopper** (*Romalea microptera*) — uncommon — toxic, can't fly; warns predators by hissing and spitting irritant foam. Yellow-and-black tank. Order: Orthoptera.
26. **Vampire Moth** (*Calyptra thalictri*) — rare — pierces fruit normally, but males drink mammalian blood. Documented to bite humans. Real, not horror movie. Order: Lepidoptera.
27. **Asian Giant Hornet / Murder Hornet** (*Vespa mandarinia*) — uncommon — 5cm body, 6mm stinger, can decapitate honey bees. Bees co-evolved a bake-it-alive defensive ball. Order: Hymenoptera.

## Bonus weird-pattern picks (3)

28. **Picture-winged Fly** (*Ceroxys urticae*) — uncommon — its wing-patterns mimic spider legs to scare away spider predators. The fly fights spiders using the SHAPE of spiders. Order: Diptera.
29. **Indian Moon Moth** (*Actias selene*) — uncommon — pale green with absurdly long tail streamers; said to confuse echolocating bats by drawing strikes to the tail, not the body. Order: Lepidoptera. (Different from Luna Moth we already have — different genus, different region.)
30. **Bee Fly** (*Bombylius major*) — uncommon — fluffy mimic of a bumblebee but actually a fly; hovers like a hummingbird, sips nectar with a needle-long proboscis. Order: Diptera.
